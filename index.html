The First Geometry-Native Foundation Model for Reality

AI is blind. We're giving it eyes.

Today's vision models are trained on flat pixels. They hallucinate depth, break down on interaction, and collapse in mission-critical domains — where fidelity, geometry, and temporal accuracy are non-negotiable.

The answer is geometry.

ChronoSpace AI is building the first Geometry-Native Foundation Model, trained on real-world 4D data: how people, objects, and environments move and interact across time.

Our North Star is clear: to reconstruct highly realistic, dynamic 4D scenes from sparse inputs — a single or few cameras — with the fidelity required for mission-critical deployment.

This unlocks true spatial intelligence: AI that understands physics, maintains temporal consistency, and generalizes across domains. Reliable systems that don't just approximate reality, but operate within it.

Geometry-native 4D data unlocks Spatial AI.

Applications

Mission-Critical Applications Demand Spatial Intelligence

Robots are trained on 4D reconstructions of real human workflows, using geometry-native content that captures affordances and motion patterns with physics built in. The same reconstructions become high-fidelity simulation environments, enabling testing and validation before deployment and closing the sim-to-real gap.

Analysts re-enter drone footage for battlefield situational awareness, transform CCTV into explorable 4D reconstructions for security and threat analysis, while immersive 4D reconstructions train personnel across missions, logistics, and equipment maintenance.

4D reconstructions create geometry-native real-world digital twins — from factory floors to warehouses and construction sites — enabling optimization, safety checks, and automation that pixels or CAD can't deliver. These same high-fidelity scenes can be replayed to test new layouts, validate automation systems, and improve safety before changes are deployed.
